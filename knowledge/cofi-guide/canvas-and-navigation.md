# Canvas and navigation

## Building on the canvas
Add a node by pressing a palette card and releasing it over the canvas. Move a node by dragging its title bar. Configure a node by clicking the gear icon on its header or double-clicking the title bar. Connect two nodes by clicking an out-port on the right of one node then an in-port on the left of another. Click a wire to cut it. Select a node and press Delete to remove it.

## Panning and zooming
Pan by dragging empty canvas. Zoom with a trackpad pinch or the mouse-wheel (it zooms toward the cursor), or use the minus / percent / plus controls at the bottom-left of the canvas.

## Fit and reset
The "fit" button frames the whole flow in the viewport in one click — useful for a wide flow you cannot see all at once. The reset button returns to 100%. Keyboard shortcuts: Shift+1 fits the flow, Home resets to 100%.

## Hiding panels
Chevron handles on the left and right edges of the canvas collapse the Ingredients panel and the Orchestrator panel so the canvas grows. Press [ or ] to toggle each panel, and press F for focus mode which hides both at once.

## Minimap
The minimap in the bottom-right corner shows the whole flow with a rectangle marking your current view. Click or drag it to jump around the canvas. Double-click it to dismiss it; a small "map" button brings it back.

## Saving and loading
Export flow downloads the whole canvas as a JSON file you can share or back up. Import flow loads one back. Save flow stores the current canvas in a browser-local library; My flows lists them and lets you either replace the canvas or insert a saved flow alongside what is already there as a reusable building block.
